DOS, Commodore-Amiga, and/or PC

  • The Ancient Art of War in the Sky
  • The Settlers 2

Sega Mega Drive (aka Sega Genesis)

  • Mega-Lo-Mania - has a great mechanic that rewards efficient play

Sony PSX

The original PlayStation was not my first console, but definitely was and still is my favourite. I cannot overstate my love of that reverb-laden boot sound. If you are running a PSX emulator, you might notice unless you provide an official bios, it doesn't include the startup sound. Be sure to fix that. Actually, I will provide some tips and useful links on emulating PSX later.

Sony also made the greatest video game TV advert ever for the PlayStation. I remember it as an advert for PS1, but looking now it was actually PS2. Here's the full short-film the advert came from: Wolfman, by Tim Hope.

  • Castlevania: Symphony of the Night
  • Crash Bandicoot (the whole series) - the first level in Crash 3 Warped is the first thing I try to get going when setting up an emulator. That music :chef's kiss:
  • Command & Conquer + Red Alert
  • Destruction Derby 2
  • Final Fantasy 7 - I was lucky enough to play this when it came out. It's such a great and iconic game, though I probably perfer the Switch version today because you can skip random battles. That said, the graphics on the Switch version are a bit jarring, with sharp 3D on blurry 2D backgrounds.
  • Spyro (the whole series)
  • Theme Hospital - The game is probably better on PC, but I've been playing the PSX port on my Vita.
  • Twisted Metal 2

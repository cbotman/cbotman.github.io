Chris Botman's homepage

Welcome to my homepage! A small place on the web I can go full-nerd. This is not a blog, we're going old-school here.

I'm an aspiring indie game developer, sharing my game(s) and some of the inspiration behind them. Hopefully this will weave together threads of interest that are well suited to sharing on the web: retro gaming (especially PS1 and PC), fantasy and sci-fi (e.g. D&D, Star Trek TNG, X-Files) and a bit of Japanese pop-culture. In other words, I'm a millennial (or Gen Y, as we used to be called) nerd, bitten by the nostalgia bug.

My homepage is not an attempt to document all of my interests or everything I do. I'm engaged in many things: being a dad and husband, hobbies (gardening, art, music, walking and reading), work, and trying to leave Earth a better place than I found it (not going well). Also, I live by the sea, which I love. But beyond this little statement, I don't really plan to talk about those things here.

Games

Super RPG Town

I'm working on a playable demo of the core game loop, so will share more when that's ready. Inspired by the gameplay of Majesty and Dragon Quest Builders, the visual style of Ultima V, the spirit of Dungeons & Dragons, and a feeling I can still remember of the first time I ever logged into an open-world MMORPG (Dransik) and took my first steps into what would become part of my life for years.

Links

Some links you might like, if you like things I also like.

Electronic Gaming Monthly (EGM) magazine scans
EGM was a popular console game magazine my older brother and I read at high school in the late '90s. Scans of every issue are available! Go back to a time before mobile phones, revisit the emergence of the web, enjoy fan art sent in the mail, but most of all, relive the glory of PSX, N64, and the unexpectedly long run of the GameBoy thanks to a small game from Japan called Pocket Monster (also Sega consoles, *sniff*).
Metal Jesus Rocks (YouTube channel)
Everybody's favourite long-haired, metal loving, retro-game collector (and pals). I decided not to collect physical games and stick to emulation, but I am happily live vicariously through Metal Jesus.
My Dribbble likes
An evergrowing list of illustrations I like on Dribbble, a portfolio site for graphic designers. There are definite themes to the style, colour palettes, and content ( irreverance, nature, and retro vibes). I like to scroll through this for inspiration.

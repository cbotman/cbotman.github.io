Chris Botman's homepage

Welcome to my homepage! A little place on the web I can go full-nerd. This is not a blog, we're going old-school here.

I'm an aspiring indie game developer, sharing my game(s) and some of the inspiration behind them. Hopefully this will weave together threads of interest well suited to sharing on the web: retro gaming (especially PS1 and PC), fantasy and sci-fi (e.g. D&D, Star Trek TNG, X-Files) and a bit of Japanese pop-culture. In other words, I'm a millennial (or Gen Y, as we used to be called) nerd, bitten by the nostalgia bug.

My homepage is not an attempt to document all of my interests or everything I do. I'm engaged in many things: being a dad and husband, hobbies (gardening, art, music, walking and reading), work, and trying to leave Earth a better place than I found it (not going well). But beyond this little statement, I don't really plan to talk about those things here.

Games

Super RPG Town

I'm working on a playable demo of the core game loop, so will share more when that's ready. Inspired by the gameplay of Majesty and Dragon Quest Builders, the visual style of old 2D tile-based RPGs such as Ultima V, the spirit of countless Dungeons & Dragons adventures, and a feeling I still remember from the first time I logged into an MMORPG (Dransik), created a character and set forwarth into a wide open world.

Links

Some links you might like, if you like things I also like.

Dave Bull (YouTube channel)
Dave is a passionate Japanese woodblock print maker. You have quite possibly seen the work that made him Internet-famous, the Ukiyo-e Heroes collab with Jed Henry. I've developed an interest in the artform as a consequence of watching and listening to Dave. Here's one of my favourite prints (link is to a gallery website Dave created): Lake Hakone in Sagami.
Electronic Gaming Monthly (EGM) magazine scans
EGM was a popular console game magazine my older brother and I read at high school in the late '90s. Scans of every issue are available! Go back to a time before mobile phones, revisit the emergence of the web, enjoy fan art sent in the mail, but most of all, relive the glory of PSX, N64, and the unexpectedly long run of the GameBoy thanks to a small game from Japan called Pocket Monster (also Sega consoles, *sniff*).
Metal Jesus Rocks (YouTube channel)
Everybody's favourite long-haired, metal loving, retro-game collector (and pals). I decided not to collect physical games and stick to emulation, but I am happily live vicariously through Metal Jesus.
